Tamagoyaki

Description

Lightly sweet yet savory, Tamagoyaki (Japanese Rolled Omelette) is flavored with dashi stock to make a delightful Japanese breakfast or side dish for your bento lunches.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon neutral flavored oil
  • Additional fillings (optional): chopped scallions, mushrooms, cheese, etc.

Step-by-step instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:

    • In a bowl, beat the eggs.
    • Add soy sauce, mirin, sugar, and salt to the beaten eggs. Mix well until combined.
    • If using additional fillings, prepare them now by chopping or grating.

  2. Cooking process:

    • Heat a rectangular Tamagoyaki pan or a small non-stick skillet over medium heat.
    • Brush the pan with a thin layer of neutral flavored oil.
    • Pour a thin layer of the egg mixture into the pan, just enough to cover the bottom.
    • Once the bottom layer of the egg starts to set but is still slightly runny on top, gently roll it up from one end of the pan to the other using chopsticks or a spatula.
    • Push the rolled omelette to the far end of the pan.
    • Add another thin layer of the egg mixture to the empty side of the pan, lifting the rolled omelette slightly to let the mixture flow underneath.
    • Once the new layer starts to set, roll it up again over the previously rolled omelette.
    • Repeat the process of adding layers and rolling until all the egg mixture is used up.
    • When the omelette is fully cooked and has a nice golden color, remove it from the pan and let it cool slightly.

  3. Serve:

    • Transfer the Tamagoyaki to a cutting board and slice it into pieces.
    • Serve warm or at room temperature as a side dish or part of a Japanese breakfast or bento box.

    4. Enjoy your homemade Tomogayaki!
